# Analyst Playbooks — what they watch and how they approach the market

A breakdown of the two analysts the dashboard is modelled on: **Mark Meldrum (MM)**
and **Defiant Gatekeeper (DG)**. Built from their actual weekly market-update
transcripts (≈3 months of digested content, both channels) plus each analyst's
explicitly stated framework. The point is the *recurring* method — the indicators
they return to every week and the lens they read them through.

> Note on coverage: Supadata's free transcript quota was exhausted at the time of
> writing, so this reflects the rolling ~3-month digest the pipeline had already
> cached rather than a fresh full-year pull. The methodology below is what repeats
> week to week, which is the part that doesn't change with a wider sample.

---

## Mark Meldrum (MM)

**One-liner:** A CFA-style, top-down macro strategist. Builds a full weekly
"state of the system" read from the Fed, the curve, liquidity plumbing, and
valuation — then assesses *probabilities*, not predictions. Closes each week with
a single-company DCF "spotlight."

### Indicators he watches (by weight in the transcripts)

1. **The Fed & rates — his #1 lens (526 mentions).**
   - **Fed funds futures (ZQ)** — appeared in 8 of 10 videos. He reads *implied
     rate-move probabilities* straight off ZQ and narrates the week-on-week shift
     ("no-hike went from 98.7% to 96.5%, so a 3.5% hike probability"). This is the
     single most distinctive thing he does.
   - **The yield curve**, specifically the **3M→10Y** steepening, and "where it
     matters for the consumer" (2Y→30Y). Watches SOFR, MBS, and the mortgage rate's
     dependence on the 10Y.
   - **Fed chair succession / Fed politics** (Warsh confirmation, Powell's term) as
     a rates catalyst.

2. **Market internals & positioning.**
   - **IWM (small caps)** — in **9 of 10 videos.** His primary breadth / risk-on
     tell. Plus **SPY**, **TLT** (duration), **REIT / IYR** (rate-sensitive), **XLU**
     (defensive rotation).

3. **Liquidity plumbing (Credit & liquidity, 168).**
   - Fed **balance-sheet runoff (QT)**, **bank reserves** (watches the ~$3T floor),
     **TGA**, **reverse repo**. Core thesis: *"when liquidity is abundant, the
     interest rate doesn't really matter anymore"* — "$6.5tn of bonds off the market
     has to go somewhere." Liquidity can hold equities up despite high rates.

4. **Valuation & earnings quality (Equities & valuation, 284).**
   - **Shiller CAPE** (explicitly "CAPE at 42"), **forward P/E** (e.g. "21× forward"),
     **forward four-quarter operating earnings**.
   - The recurring **"exactly like 1999"** dot-com analogy.
   - **"CFO illusions"** — earnings quality: free cash flow vs net income, stock-based
     comp, and CapEx-driven revenue recognition inflating reported growth.

5. **Inflation.** CPI **month-over-month decomposition**, stripping energy to find
   the core trend; breakevens.

6. **Labor & growth quality.** NFP *composition* (e.g. healthcare-heavy prints),
   nonfarm productivity, and **GDP quality** — distinguishing low-quality GDP
   (inventories/government) from **real final sales**. Jobless claims, ISM.

7. **Commodities.** **WTI vs Brent spread**, oil as an *exogenous, rate-insensitive*
   driver, gold.

8. **Geopolitics — huge (571 mentions).** Tariffs/Trump, Iran & the Strait of Hormuz,
   China/Xi, war-footing budgets. He treats geopolitics as the swing factor for oil
   and the risk regime rather than a side topic.

### How he approaches the market
- **Probabilistic, not predictive** — "I'm assessing probabilities," reads the
  *regime* (risk-on vs risk-off) rather than calling tops/bottoms.
- **The sell signal he watches for is the Fed pivoting from cutting to hiking**, not
  the first cut.
- **Liquidity-regime thinker**: high valuations are tolerable while liquidity is
  ample; the risk is liquidity *withdrawal*.
- **Valuation-aware and generally cautious** (CAPE 42, the 1999 parallel, CFO
  illusions), but won't fight an abundant-liquidity tape.
- **Bottom-up cross-check**: a weekly **DCF spotlight company** to ground the macro.

## Defiant Gatekeeper (DG)

**One-liner:** A distilled, checklist-driven macro investor. Anchors every week on
*two* data points, runs a fixed "5-box" regime checklist, and reads the AI/semi
complex through forward multiples and the equity risk premium. More
beginner-oriented and more compressed than MM.

### Indicators he watches

1. **His stated weekly core — two data points, every week:**
   > "The two most important data we monitor on a weekly basis are **(1) the oil
   > price** and **(2) the 10-year Treasury bond yield**."
   Everything else is read in their context.

2. **The "5-box" regime checklist (his "macro investing 101").**
   - A fixed set of conditions — *"if those five boxes are checked, especially in the
     post-2008 QE era, you make money most of the time."* A liquidity-regime,
     buy-the-beta framework gated by macro conditions.

3. **Fed & rates — his #1 theme (166 mentions).**
   - Fed funds path and **market-implied rate-cut odds** ("over 50%… one cut by
     year-end"), and the **balance sheet → long end** link ("QT means they're not
     buying long bonds, so the 10Y rises").

4. **Valuation via forward multiples (Equities & valuation, 149).**
   - Leans heavily on **forward P/E and 2-year-forward (2027) multiples**, especially
     for semis — he quotes them by name (AMD ~63× fwd / ~150× trailing, Intel ~110×,
     Marvell ~52×).
   - **Equity risk premium** (~8%) and the implied **earnings yield** ("stocks should
     trade at ~12.5%") to judge whether prices are justified.
   - Skeptical of **CapEx-justified AI valuations** — asks whether public shareholders
     actually benefit from the spend.

5. **VIX as the fear gauge.** Explicit bands: **"15–20 = calm, uptrend."** Uses it to
   read complacency vs stress.

6. **Inflation.** **Michigan 1-year inflation expectations** (flagged 4.8%) and CPI
   vs consensus (3.8% vs 3.7% expected).

7. **Credit & liquidity (55).** Credit-crunch mechanics — junk paying a **15–20%
   premium over risk-free** when stress hits; the **"Fed + Trump put"** (policy
   support) as a floor.

8. **Geopolitics (98).** Notably the **UK gilt / sovereign-debt spillover** into US
   Treasuries — rates contagion across sovereigns.

9. **Bubble watch.** Recurring "is this a bubble?" framing across asset classes
   (semis, even Pokémon cards) — tests froth against the liquidity backdrop.

### How he approaches the market
- **Checklist / regime-first**: two key weekly data + a 5-box gate. Simpler and more
  mechanical than MM's broad dashboard.
- **Liquidity-driven, post-2008-QE worldview** — "you make money when the boxes are
  checked." Buy beta while the regime holds.
- **Valuation as the brake**: forward multiples + equity-risk-premium math tell him
  when the AI/semi complex has run too far.
- **Educational, accessible tone** ("as I tell you all the time"), recently
  **cautious** ("be cautious," "you are not smart… be humble").

## Side-by-side

| Dimension | Mark Meldrum | Defiant Gatekeeper |
|---|---|---|
| **Style** | Broad weekly macro dashboard | Distilled 2-data + 5-box checklist |
| **Fed read** | ZQ-implied probabilities, curve internals | Implied cut-odds, QT → long-end |
| **Anchor data** | Whole system (curve, liquidity, CAPE, labor) | **Oil + 10Y**, every week |
| **Valuation** | CAPE, fwd P/E, CFO illusions, 1999 analogy | Fwd & 2yr-fwd P/E, equity risk premium |
| **Liquidity** | TGA / RRP / reserves plumbing, QT | Fed balance sheet + "Fed/Trump put" |
| **Sentiment** | IWM breadth, defensive rotation | VIX bands (15–20) |
| **Geopolitics** | Heavy (oil + risk regime) | Moderate (rates contagion, e.g. UK gilts) |
| **Bottom-up** | Weekly DCF spotlight | Forward-multiple call-outs on semis |
| **Core belief** | Regime > prediction; liquidity can override rates | Buy beta while the boxes are checked; valuation is the brake |

**Where they agree:** both are top-down and macro-first; both anchor on the **Fed and
the 10-year**; both are **liquidity-regime** thinkers (abundant liquidity supports
risk); both are **valuation-aware** and both are actively watching the **AI/semi
bubble through forward multiples**.

**Where they differ:** MM runs a *wider, more granular* board (curve plumbing,
liquidity internals, GDP quality, labor composition, credit OAS, CAPE, earnings
quality) and is explicitly *probabilistic* off ZQ. DG runs a *tighter, more
mechanical* process (two weekly data + a 5-box gate + VIX bands + equity-risk-premium
math) aimed at a less technical audience.

## How the dashboard already maps to this

The tracker covers most of their shared lens already:

- **Fed:** Fed Watch tab (implied cut/hike odds via the 3M T-bill vs fed funds —
  MM's ZQ read), FOMC calendar.
- **Rates/curve:** 2s10s, the full Treasury curve, real 10Y.
- **Liquidity:** net liquidity (WALCL − TGA − RRP), reserves — MM's plumbing.
- **Valuation:** Shiller CAPE, forward P/E, **FY28 P/E**, CFO-illusion flags
  (FCF/NI, SBC %OCF) — both analysts' valuation lens.
- **Internals:** IWM (small caps), KRE (banks), defensive ETFs, HYG/TLT.
- **Growth quality:** real GDP vs final sales, CFNAI, Empire/Philly.
- **Commodities:** WTI/Brent, gold, dollar.

**Closest gaps to their playbooks** (candidates to add): a **VIX regime band** (DG's
15–20), an explicit **equity risk premium / earnings-yield-minus-10Y** readout (DG),
and **ISM manufacturing & services** as standalone tiles (both reference ISM
directly).
